# Webinar Funnel Teardown

> Reverse-engineer a webinar/masterclass funnel end to end — registration, the
> pitch structure, the offer, and the follow-up — and find where it leaks.

## What it does

Breaks down a webinar-based sales funnel (live or evergreen masterclass, VSL, or
challenge that ends in a pitch) into its stages: the registration page, show-up
and reminder mechanics, the webinar's content→transition→offer structure, the
offer with its stack and urgency, and the post-webinar email/retargeting
sequence. Ends with the biggest leaks and how to fix them. Pairs well with
[online-school-audit](../online-school-audit/SKILL.md) and
[offer-teardown](../offer-teardown/SKILL.md).

## When to use this skill

- "Break down this webinar / masterclass funnel: <url>."
- "Reverse-engineer <creator>'s webinar."
- "Analyze this free training that pitches a course at the end."
- Competitive research on course/coaching launch funnels.

## Inputs needed

- **Required:** the registration URL (and the sales/offer page if separate).
- **Helpful:** whether it's live or evergreen, the niche, and — importantly —
  **notes from the user if they attended** (the webinar content itself and the
  pitch usually aren't observable from the page alone).
- **Be honest about access:** you can analyze the registration page, confirmation/
  reminder emails (if provided), and the offer page. The live webinar content is
  often not observable — say so, and ask the user what they saw if they attended.

## Method

1. **Registration page** — the hook/promise, the "what you'll learn" bullets, the
   date/time or "instant access" (live vs evergreen tell), scarcity, and how much
   friction the opt-in has. Grade its clarity and desire.
2. **Show-up mechanics** — confirmation page, reminder emails/SMS, replay window,
   "add to calendar", and any incentive to attend live. (Analyze what's provided.)
3. **Webinar structure** (from user notes/recording if available) — map it against
   the classic pattern: hook → story/credibility → 3 content "secrets" (teach what,
   not how) → transition to the offer → value stack → price reveal & anchor →
   guarantee → urgency/scarcity → Q&A/close. Note which beats are present.
4. **The offer** — what's sold, price, payment plans, bonuses/stack, guarantee,
   and the urgency mechanism (cart close, fast-action bonus, seat limit).
5. **Post-webinar follow-up** — the email/retargeting sequence that closes
   non-buyers (replay, objection-handling, deadline, last-call). Analyze if provided.
6. **Find the leaks** — registration conversion, show-up rate, the content→pitch
   transition, offer clarity, or a weak follow-up. Identify the biggest one.

## Quality bar

- Clearly separate what you observed (registration/offer pages, provided emails)
  from what's inferred or came from the user's attendance notes.
- Do not fabricate the webinar script or the offer if you couldn't see them — mark
  as "not observed" and note what's needed.
- Flag manipulative tactics (fake live/"just started", perma-urgency, unverifiable
  income claims) honestly rather than praising them.
- No invented attendance, conversion, or revenue numbers.

## Common mistakes to avoid

- Reviewing only the registration page and calling it a funnel teardown.
- Presenting the inferred webinar structure as if you watched it.
- Ignoring the post-webinar follow-up, where most sales actually close.
