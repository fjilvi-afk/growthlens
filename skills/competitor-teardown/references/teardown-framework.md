# Competitor Teardown — the nine lenses (reference)

Detailed prompts for each lens. Load this when you need depth on a specific
section. The goal is always the same: reconstruct strategy from public evidence,
and keep observed facts separate from inferences.

## 1. Snapshot

- Company name, the category they operate in, and a one-sentence "what they do".
- Stage/size signals: team/about page, "we're hiring", funding/press mentions,
  number and tier of customer logos, year founded.
- Geography and language(s) served.

## 2. Positioning

- The **hero promise**: the first headline + subhead. Write it down verbatim.
- The **category** they place themselves in ("the X for Y").
- The **differentiator** they lean on (speed, price, ease, depth, niche focus).
- The **enemy/alternative** they position against ("unlike spreadsheets…").
- Tone and sophistication level of the messaging.

Frameworks worth applying: April Dunford's positioning (competitive alternatives →
unique attributes → value → who cares most), and the "promise + proof + CTA" read
of the hero.

## 3. Target audience / ICP

- Roles, industries, company sizes named in copy.
- Segments addressed by separate pages ("for startups", "for agencies").
- Jobs-to-be-done implied by the headline benefits.

## 4. Offer & value ladder

- Entry points: lead magnet, free tool, free trial, freemium tier.
- Core offer: the main paid product/plan.
- Ascension: upsells, add-ons, higher tiers, services, community.
- Map it as a ladder: free → low-ticket → core → premium.

## 5. Pricing signals

- Published prices, currency, billing cadence (monthly/annual), per-seat vs flat.
- Packaging: number of tiers, what gates each tier, the "most popular" anchor.
- Psychological pricing: anchoring, decoy tier, annual discount, urgency.
- If pricing is "contact sales", note it — implies higher ACV / sales-led motion.

## 6. Funnel & conversion path

- Primary CTA on the homepage and how it repeats.
- The path: landing → signup/demo/checkout → activation.
- Lead capture: email gates, gated content, exit intent.
- Friction points and trust reducers along the way.

## 7. Acquisition channels (evidence-based)

- **SEO**: blog depth, topic clusters, glossary/resource pages, programmatic pages.
- **Paid**: dedicated landing pages, "ad" style hero, UTM parameters in links.
- **Social/community**: linked profiles, community, Slack/Discord, webinars.
- **Partnerships/affiliates**: integrations pages, partner/affiliate programs.
- Be explicit that this is inference from on-site evidence, not ad-spend data.

## 8. Social proof & trust

- Testimonials (named vs anonymous), case studies with numbers, logos.
- Third-party reviews referenced (G2, Trustpilot, app stores).
- Guarantees, security/compliance badges, money-back terms.

## 9. Strengths & vulnerabilities

- Strengths: what is genuinely well-executed and hard to copy.
- Vulnerabilities: thin proof, confusing pricing, narrow ICP, weak funnel step,
  missing channel, dated messaging.
- Convert the biggest vulnerability into one concrete opening for a challenger.
