# Online School Playbook — funnel patterns & what to look for (reference)

Load this when you need depth on the funnel and economics of education
businesses. It lists the common funnel archetypes and the signals to look for in
each block of the audit.

## Funnel archetypes (identify which one)

1. **Lead magnet → email nurture → sales page** — free PDF/checklist/mini-course
   captures email; an email sequence sells the core offer. Evergreen.
2. **Webinar / masterclass funnel** — free (or low-cost) live or evergreen
   webinar that teaches + pitches at the end. Look for "register", countdowns,
   "replay expires", and a special offer for attendees.
3. **VSL (video sales letter)** — a long-form video on the sales page does the
   selling; CTA to buy or book a call.
4. **Challenge funnel** — a 5-day / 7-day free or low-ticket challenge builds
   commitment, then pitches the program at the end.
5. **Application / book-a-call** — high-ticket coaching; the "offer" is a call.
   Look for qualification questions and scarcity of slots.
6. **Organic → DM / community** — content on YouTube/IG/TikTok drives to a free
   community or DM, sold via relationship. Hard to see fully from the site.

The conversion event differs by archetype (purchase, application, booked call).
Always state which archetype and where the money decision happens.

## Offer / value ladder signals

- Free: lead magnet, free webinar, free community, free trial.
- Low-ticket / tripwire: a small first purchase ($7–$97 range) or a workshop.
- Core: the flagship course/cohort (the main revenue driver).
- High-ticket: mastermind, 1:1 coaching, done-with-you, certification.
- Continuity: membership, community subscription, "alumni" tier.

## Pricing & payment signals

- Price tiers, pay-in-full vs installments, pay-in-full bonus.
- Anchoring: "normally $X, today $Y", value-stack totals.
- Urgency: cart open/close dates, cohort start dates, bonus expiry, seat limits.
- Scholarships/discounts and how they're framed.

## Proof signals (and red flags)

- Strong: named students with specific, verifiable outcomes; case studies; third-
  party reviews; transparent refund/guarantee terms.
- Weak / red flags: anonymous testimonials, income screenshots, "results not
  typical" fine print, fake countdown timers, perma-"closing soon", unverifiable
  earnings claims. Note these; do not treat them as evidence.

## Traffic engine signals

- SEO: blog, YouTube library, podcast, free tools, glossary.
- Paid: dedicated landing pages, ad-library presence, retargeting.
- Borrowed audience: podcast guesting, affiliates, JV launches, partnerships.
- Identify the *primary* engine and how dependent it is on one channel or the
  founder's personal brand.

## Retention / fulfillment signals

- Cohort vs self-paced; live calls; community platform (Circle, Skool, Discord,
  Slack, Telegram); coaching/support; completion mechanics; alumni/continuity.
- Retention matters most for membership/continuity models — weak community or
  low completion is a vulnerability.

## Growth model read

- Launch-based (open/close cart, big spikes) vs evergreen (always-on funnel).
- Primary growth lever (content, ads, affiliates, partnerships, referrals).
- Founder-brand dependence (a key vulnerability if the brand = one person).
- Where a challenger could attack: thin proof, single-channel dependence, weak
  retention, an unclear or undifferentiated transformation.
